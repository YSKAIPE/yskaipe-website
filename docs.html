Overview

YSKAIPE is a home services cost intelligence platform. Our AutoQuote tool uses Claude AI (by Anthropic) to generate instant standard cost estimates for home repair and improvement jobs based on verified NC 2026 industry rates.

The goal is simple: give every homeowner the knowledge they need to make a fair decision before any work begins. Whether you want to DIY, negotiate with a contractor, or just know if a quote is reasonable — YSKAIPE gives you the baseline.

Current Coverage
North Carolina residential market · 2026 verified rates · Labor baseline $95–$160/hr · 14 trade categories

How to Use

Step 1 — Describe your job

Navigate to the AutoQuote section. Select your trade, enter your ZIP code, and describe the job in plain English. The more detail you provide, the more accurate your estimate will be.

Step 2 — Get your estimate

YSKAIPE returns a standard cost estimate within seconds, broken down into labor and materials ranges, with a plain-English explanation of what drives the cost.

Step 3 — Choose your path

From your estimate, you can go two directions:

  • DIY This Job — get step-by-step instructions, a difficulty rating, safety warnings, and a materials list with retailer links
  • Find a Pro — see matched local professionals and the fair rate to expect from them

Rate Methodology

YSKAIPE estimates are based on verified NC 2026 residential trade rates, compiled from industry data, regional labor surveys, and material cost indexes.

Labor rates

Our labor baseline is $95–$160/hr depending on trade, complexity, and region within NC. Specialty trades (HVAC, electrical, EV infrastructure) sit at the higher end. General labor and painting typically fall at the lower end.

Materials

Material costs are estimated using current retail pricing from major home improvement suppliers. These figures reflect what a homeowner would pay at retail — contractors may pay less wholesale.

Regional adjustment

The Claude AI model applies regional context within NC — Charlotte metro, Triangle, Triad, and coastal markets each have slight cost variations that are factored into estimates when a ZIP code is provided.

How to use your estimate
If a contractor's quote is within ~20% of the YSKAIPE total, it's likely fair. More than 30% above? Ask for itemized breakdown. Significantly below? Ask what's not included.

DIY Report

When you click DIY This Job, YSKAIPE generates a full DIY playbook including:

  • Difficulty rating — scored 1–10 with a plain label (Easy / Weekend Warrior / Experienced DIYer / Expert Only)
  • Safety & permit warnings — flagged prominently for any job involving electrical, plumbing, structural, or permitted work
  • Step-by-step instructions — numbered, plain English, tool-specific guidance
  • Materials list — specific product names, quantities, and estimated costs
  • Shop links — direct retailer search links for every material item

DIY savings shown reflect the labor cost avoided — materials still apply.

Shop Materials

Every DIY report includes a Shop Materials section that links each required material directly to Amazon, Home Depot, and Lowe's search results.

Links are generated automatically from the AI's materials list — no manual curation. Product names are optimized for retail search to surface the most relevant results.

Claude AI

YSKAIPE's cost intelligence is powered by Claude, developed by Anthropic. Claude is one of the most capable and safety-focused AI models available. We chose Anthropic as our AI partner because their values — honesty, transparency, and genuine helpfulness — align directly with YSKAIPE's mission.

Accuracy & Limits

YSKAIPE estimates are reference figures, not binding quotes. Actual costs vary based on:

  • Job complexity not captured in the description
  • Material price fluctuations
  • Contractor overhead, markup, and availability
  • Permit fees, disposal costs, and unforeseen complications
  • Geographic variation within NC beyond ZIP-level adjustment

In our testing, YSKAIPE estimates fall within 15–25% of actual contractor quotes for clearly described standard jobs. Complex or ambiguous jobs will have wider ranges.

Tips for Better Quotes

  • Include the age of your home — older homes often have non-standard plumbing, wiring, or dimensions that affect cost
  • Describe symptoms, not just the fix — "water dripping under sink" is better than "fix leak"
  • Mention any known constraints — access issues, existing damage, HOA restrictions
  • Specify the scope — "replace one faucet" vs "replumb entire kitchen" yields very different estimates
  • Select the correct trade — a general contractor quote differs significantly from a specialist
