Peaking Waters
Blowing Rock, NC • Blue Ridge Mountains
Permaculture Food Forest • Regenerative Development

Hands-on transformation of a steep, rhododendron-covered ridge at 215 Edmisten Road / Ski Mountain Rd into a private mountain sanctuary and thriving native food forest in the High Country of North Carolina.

Completed Phase 1 infrastructure with zero imported materials: 400-foot deep well system for reliable water, extensive terracing and earthwork, new driveways, and hand-built wooden walkways — all carved from the land itself.

Every rock unearthed (billion-year-old Blowing Rock Gneiss and slate) is repurposed into dry-stack retaining walls, crushed-stone paths, and hugelkultur beds — true incremental, sustainable permaculture.

Living Native Food Forest in Progress
Already thriving:
Great Laurel thickets, towering Fraser Firs, oaks, hickories, serviceberry, wild blueberries, ramps
Expanding this spring:
Pawpaw, Elderberry, American Hazelnut, Spicebush, highbush blueberry, wild strawberry + cool-season crops (Kennebec potatoes, kale, collards, beets)
