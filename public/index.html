How it works

Simple enough to explain
in three sentences.

Join a pod around something worth doing. Help three people do it. Receive help from three people in return. That's the whole deal.

STEP 01
Find a pod that fits
Browse the open pods — gardening, baking, Bitcoin, coding, whatever's next — and join the one that fits your interest. Reply to the pinned post or DM @yskaipe. No form, no algorithm.
→
STEP 02
Get matched into a triad
The pod coordinator matches you with two or three others based on interest, location, and what you can offer. That's your triad — three people, one shared pursuit, a clear reciprocity contract.
→
STEP 03
Help three. Receive from three.
Give hands-on help to your triad members — planting, baking, debugging, reviewing. In return, expect the same from them when you need it. Balanced support. No free riders.
→
STEP 04
Share the yield
Share what the pod produces — loaves, harvests, prototypes, knowledge — with those who helped you make it. What you give, compounds. Abundance cycles outperform solo hoarding every time.
→

The Social Contract

Simple rules.
No free riders.

YSKAIPE runs on one idea: reciprocity actually works in small groups. The contract is short enough to say out loud in twenty seconds.

  • 01
    Help three people. Give hands-on help to three triad members with their actual work — not advice, not cheering. Real help. Planting, baking, debugging, building.
  • 02
    Receive from three people. When you need it, expect the same back. The contract is balanced and tit-for-tat. No one left alone with a problem they can't crack solo.
  • 03
    Share the yield. Whatever you produce — grapes, loaves, code, leads — share proportionally with those who helped you build it. Abundance cycles outperform solo hoarding every time.
  • 04
    Show up. Triads work because everyone turns up. If you can commit to Wednesdays and Fridays for four weeks, you belong here.
