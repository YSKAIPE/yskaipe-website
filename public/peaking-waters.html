A mountain retreat & permaculture sanctuary in the making

Peaking Waters sits on a steep, beautiful ridge near Blowing Rock/Boone, North Carolina (36°09'50"N 81°39'46"W). Dense Great Laurel (Rosebay Rhododendron) thickets, towering Fraser Firs, hardwoods, and sweeping views define the land.

We've been doing all the heavy lifting ourselves — clearing, grading, digging, and building the backbone so this land can become a peaceful private sanctuary, glamping spot, and fully self-sustaining native food forest.

The property's ancient Blowing Rock Gneiss and slate bedrock is being excavated and immediately repurposed — zero-mile sustainable infrastructure that will support terraces, paths, walls, and hugelkultur beds for decades to come.

Living System in Progress

The Native Food Forest & Permaculture Vision

Already thriving: dense Great Laurel thickets, Fraser Firs, hardwoods, and layered native edibles. We're expanding with new plantings and using every excavated rock for sustainability. More to come as we achieve full self-reliance through permaculture.

Existing Native Forest Layers

  • • Canopy: Oaks, hickories, tulip poplars, and Fraser Firs (structure, nuts, wildlife habitat, aromatic shelter)
  • • Understory: Serviceberry, pawpaw, persimmon, elderberry, wild plum, spicebush
  • • Shrub Layer: Highbush/lowbush blueberries, raspberries/blackberries, American hazelnut — all thriving in acidic mountain soils
  • • Groundcover & Herbs: Wild strawberries, ramps (in moist shade), violets, Jerusalem artichokes, mushrooms
  • • Signature Understory: Impenetrable Great Laurel thickets (ecological powerhouse for erosion control, pollinators, and winter cover)

Water Independence

Mountain Spring Water

Pure, gravity-fed mountain water is the lifeblood of Peaking Waters. Our deep well taps into ancient aquifers, delivering clean, mineral-rich water reminiscent of natural springs — reliable year-round, even at elevation.

System Highlights

  • • 400-ft deep well with submersible pump — high-volume flow for irrigation, glamping, and future expansion
  • • Custom wellhouse protects infrastructure from freezing mountain winters
  • • Clean, cold, naturally filtered water — soft, low-mineral profile ideal for plants, drinking, and off-grid living
  • • Future plans: gravity-fed cisterns, rainwater harvesting integration, and spring-like catchment enhancements

Secure, high-elevation water source — powering the ridge's permaculture future.

Efficient Warmth

Rocket Mass Heating

Planning ultra-efficient rocket mass heaters — clean-burning, wood-saving systems that turn twigs and branches into long-lasting radiant heat. Perfect for mountain winters and full off-grid self-reliance.

Why Rocket Mass Heaters?

  • • Burns 1/4 the wood of conventional stoves — uses small branches, scraps, and on-site biomass
  • • Near-complete combustion — ultra-clean exhaust (mostly steam and CO₂), minimal smoke/creosote
  • • Thermal mass (cob/earth bench) stores heat for 12–24+ hours of gentle radiant warmth
  • • Integrates with permaculture: heats future structures, greenhouses, or drying areas sustainably

Next phase: Build one in the bunk cabin or central gathering space — efficient, beautiful, and aligned with zero-import ethos.

Core Principles

Insulated combustion chamber reaches high temps for clean burn.

Heat riser creates rocket-like draft and afterburn of gases.

Mass bench (earthen/cob) absorbs and slowly radiates heat.

Future build: low-fuel, high-comfort heating for the mountains.
